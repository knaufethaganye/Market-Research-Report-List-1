<p><p>The reagent bottle market encompasses two primary types: glass and plastic. Glass reagent bottles are favored for their chemical resistance, compatibility with solvents, and durability, making them ideal for long-term storage and laboratory use. However, they are heavier and more fragile. In contrast, plastic reagent bottles, typically made from materials like polyethylene or polypropylene, offer lightweight options that are resistant to breakage. They are often more cost-effective and suitable for single-use applications, catering to diverse laboratory and industrial needs.</p></p>
